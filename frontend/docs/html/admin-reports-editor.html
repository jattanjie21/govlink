Where The Gambia's healthcare access actually breaks down.

Mapping facility distance against population density across 120 wards reveals what averages have been hiding for a decade — and the regions where the next health-sector budget could move the most.

The averages problem

The Ministry of Health's headline figure for facility access has barely moved in five years. In 2019, the average Gambian lived 6.4 km from the nearest healthcare facility. In 2024, that average is 6.1 km — a 4.7% improvement that, if you stop reading there, looks like quiet progress. But averages, as anyone who has waited three hours at a rural health post will tell you, can be the most misleading number in a national dataset.

The 2024 Health Facility Census is the first national register that pairs every one of The Gambia's 728 healthcare facilities with a verified geocoded location.

Where the gap actually is

The clearest way to see the picture is the scatter at the top of this report. Plotting facility distance against population density places every ward on a grid where the policy goal sits in the lower-right.

The headline 6.1 km figure is not wrong. It is just an average across two countries — the urbanised South-West, and the eastern wards where access has stagnated since the late 2000s. — Dr. Lamin Sanyang, Director of Public Health, MoH
